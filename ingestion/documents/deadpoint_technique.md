# Deadpoint Technique

A deadpoint is a controlled dynamic climbing movement in which the climber reaches for the next hold near the moment when upward momentum slows.

The technique can help a climber reach holds that are difficult to access statically without requiring a fully explosive jump.

A successful deadpoint usually involves coordinated leg drive, hip movement, accurate timing, and a relaxed reaching arm.

Practice should begin on comfortable holds and predictable moves. The goal is to improve timing and accuracy rather than simply pulling harder with the arms.
