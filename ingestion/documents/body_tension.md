# Body Tension on Overhanging Routes

Body tension is the ability to keep the feet connected to the wall while force moves through the hands, core, hips, and legs.

On steep climbing, poor body tension often causes the feet to cut away from the wall. This increases the load on the arms and makes the next movement harder.

Useful body-tension techniques include keeping the hips close to the wall, pressing actively through the toes, using opposing pressure between the hands and feet, and engaging the core before initiating a move.

Training methods include hollow-body holds, front planks, controlled overhang climbing, and exercises that require maintaining foot contact during long reaches.
